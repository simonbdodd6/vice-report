What matters in a TV for GTA 6

  • HDMI 2.1 inputs. Full-bandwidth HDMI 2.1 lets a PS5 or Series X output 4K at up to 120Hz. Look for at least two 2.1 ports so a console and a soundbar or capture card can coexist.
  • VRR (Variable Refresh Rate). Open-world games have fluctuating frame rates, and VRR smooths out those dips without tearing. Rockstar has not yet confirmed GTA 6's performance modes, so VRR is your insurance policy either way.
  • Low input lag and ALLM. A good game mode under ~10 ms at 60Hz keeps driving and shooting responsive; Auto Low Latency Mode switches to it automatically.
  • HDR performance. Vice City at night is exactly the kind of neon-on-dark content where OLED contrast or a strong Mini-LED local-dimming implementation earns its keep.

One honest caveat: as of this writing, Rockstar has not published GTA 6's resolution or frame-rate targets. Treat any claim about "GTA 6 at 120fps" as unconfirmed — the specs below future-proof you regardless of what the final modes turn out to be.

Picture-quality pick for dark rooms: LG C-series OLED

If you play in a dark or light-controlled room and want the best image GTA 6 can produce, OLED remains the one to beat: per-pixel contrast that flatters neon-lit night scenes, near-instant pixel response, four full HDMI 2.1 ports, and class-leading input lag. The trade-offs are lower peak brightness than Mini-LED and some long-term image-retention risk with static HUD elements over very long sessions — worth accepting for the picture, if your room suits it.

  • OLED panel with self-lit pixels — true blacks, no blooming
  • 4K 120Hz with VRR (HDMI Forum, G-Sync and FreeSync)
  • Four full-bandwidth HDMI 2.1 inputs

Which one should you buy?

For most people: the Samsung QN90-series. It matches how GTA 6 will actually get played — long sessions, a static HUD, rooms that are not always dark — with the brightness to fight glare and none of OLED's burn-in caveats. If you play in a dark room and want the absolute best image, the LG C-series OLED trades those practicalities for perfect blacks. Just want the gaming essentials without flagship spend: the Hisense U7-series covers HDMI 2.1, VRR and low lag — everything GTA 6 is likely to reward — for the least money.
