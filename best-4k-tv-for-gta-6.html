What matters in a TV for GTA 6

  • HDMI 2.1 inputs. Full-bandwidth HDMI 2.1 lets a PS5 or Series X output 4K at up to 120Hz. Look for at least two 2.1 ports so a console and a soundbar or capture card can coexist.
  • VRR (Variable Refresh Rate). Open-world games have fluctuating frame rates, and VRR smooths out those dips without tearing. Rockstar has not yet confirmed GTA 6's performance modes, so VRR is your insurance policy either way.
  • Low input lag and ALLM. A good game mode under ~10 ms at 60Hz keeps driving and shooting responsive; Auto Low Latency Mode switches to it automatically.
  • HDR performance. Vice City at night is exactly the kind of neon-on-dark content where OLED contrast or a strong Mini-LED local-dimming implementation earns its keep.

One honest caveat: as of this writing, Rockstar has not published GTA 6's resolution or frame-rate targets. Treat any claim about "GTA 6 at 120fps" as unconfirmed — the specs below future-proof you regardless of what the final modes turn out to be.

Our picks

For a game built around neon-lit Vice City nights, OLED is the most noticeable single upgrade you can make.

Best for bright rooms: Samsung QN90-series Neo QLED

If your living room gets a lot of daylight, a bright Mini-LED set is the more practical choice. Samsung's QN90 line pairs aggressive full-array local dimming with very high peak brightness, so daytime driving through Leonida still pops with the curtains open, and there is no risk of the permanent image retention some people worry about with OLED.

  • Mini-LED backlight with high peak brightness for bright rooms
  • 4K 120Hz, VRR and ALLM across its HDMI 2.1 inputs
  • Strong anti-glare coating
  • Game Bar overlay for quick lag/VRR settings

Which one should you buy?

Dark or light-controlled room and you want the best image GTA 6 can produce: get the LG C-series OLED. Bright, sunny living room: the Samsung QN90-series trades OLED's perfect blacks for the brightness to fight glare. Just want the gaming essentials without flagship spend: the Hisense U7-series covers HDMI 2.1, VRR and low lag — everything GTA 6 is likely to reward — for the least money.
