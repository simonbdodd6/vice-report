GTA 6 Map Size vs GTA 5: What We Know So Far

Last updated: July 5, 2026

One of the biggest questions ahead of GTA 6's launch is simple: how big is the map going to be, and how does it stack up against GTA 5's Los Santos and Blaine County? Here's a grounded look at what's actually confirmed versus what's still speculation, leak-derived, or fan estimate.

What GTA 5's map actually offers, for context

GTA 5 shipped with a combined city-and-countryside map covering Los Santos and the surrounding Blaine County region, including desert, mountains, farmland, and an ocean floor players could explore. It was, at release, one of the largest open worlds in the genre, and it has remained a benchmark that every subsequent open-world game gets measured against.

What Rockstar has officially confirmed about GTA 6

Rockstar's official trailers confirm the game is set in a fictional version of Florida, centered on Vice City, with additional areas glimpsed including smaller towns, swampland, beaches, and highways connecting different biomes. Rockstar has not released any official statement about total map size, square mileage, or a direct size comparison to GTA 5 or any other title. Anything beyond what appears on-screen in official marketing should be treated as unconfirmed.

Unconfirmed rumors and leaks — labeled clearly

Various leaks, datamines, and fan analyses circulating since GTA 6's announcement have claimed the map could be multiple times larger than GTA 5's. None of these figures have been verified by Rockstar Games, and leak-sourced size claims have a long history of being wrong or exaggerated in past GTA release cycles. Treat any specific number you see online — whether in square miles or as a multiplier of GTA 5's map — as rumor, not fact, until Rockstar confirms it directly.

Why size comparisons are hard to verify right now

Trailers are edited for pacing and don't reveal the full playable boundary of a map. Leaked assets or map files, when they surface, are often incomplete, outdated, or reference test environments rather than the shipped game. Until Rockstar publishes official details or the game is available to explore directly, precise comparisons remain guesswork.

What seems reasonably likely, without overstating it

Based on the confirmed trailer footage alone, GTA 6 appears to include a mix of dense urban environment (Vice City itself) plus rural and coastal regions outside it, similar in structure to how GTA 5 balanced Los Santos against Blaine County. Whether the overall footprint is larger, smaller, or similar in scale to GTA 5 is not something Rockstar has stated, so no firm conclusion can be drawn yet.

Frequently asked questions

Has Rockstar confirmed the GTA 6 map size?

No. Rockstar Games has not published official square-mileage figures or size comparisons for GTA 6's map. Any numbers circulating online are estimates or leaks, not confirmed facts.

Is GTA 6 bigger than GTA 5?

It's widely expected to be larger given the trailers show multiple distinct regions, but this remains unconfirmed. Rockstar has not stated an official size comparison to GTA 5's map.

Will GTA 6 include areas outside Vice City?

Trailer footage shows swampland, small towns, and coastal areas beyond the main city, suggesting a broader Florida-inspired setting. Rockstar has not detailed the full extent of these areas.

The bottom line

GTA 5's map remains the only confirmed reference point we can measure against, since Rockstar has not disclosed GTA 6's actual size. Until an official statement, gameplay reveal, or the game's release provides verified details, treat every "leaked" size figure as unconfirmed rumor rather than fact. We'll update this page as soon as Rockstar shares anything official.
